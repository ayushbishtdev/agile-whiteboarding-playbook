# The 48-Hour Async Retrospective Checklist

Use this checklist to decouple ideation from decision-making, preventing time-zone exclusion and senior-engineer groupthink.

## T-Minus 48 Hours: Setup & Distribution
- [ ] Create a new whiteboard instance (or duplicate your sprint template).
- [ ] Lock down all structural elements (frames, headers) so participants don't break the template.
- [ ] Add explicit, written instructions to the top of the board defining the sprint scope.
- [ ] Distribute the link to the distributed team via Slack/Teams.
- [ ] **Rule Enforcement:** State clearly that participants are to add items silently. No commenting, clustering, or debating yet.
- [ ] **Leadership alignment:** Ask the Product Owner or Tech Lead to add the first 2-3 notes to model the expected behavior.

## T-Minus 24 Hours: The Nudge
- [ ] Send an automated ping in your team's chat application reminding them to contribute.
- [ ] Verify that team members in offset time zones (e.g., APAC/EMEA) have logged in and dropped their blockers.

## T-Minus 4 Hours: The Cutoff
- [ ] Send the final reminder. 
- [ ] Close the board for new asynchronous input to prevent last-minute additions that the team hasn't digested.

## Live Ceremony: 30-Minute Synchronous Sprint
- [ ] **0:00 - 0:05 | Context:** Open the populated board. Acknowledge the inputs without reading every single card aloud.
- [ ] **0:05 - 0:15 | Clustering:** Group similar sticky notes into overarching themes collaboratively. (Leverage native AI clustering tools if approved by Infosec).
- [ ] **0:15 - 0:20 | Voting:** Give the team 3 votes each to identify the highest-priority themes to tackle.
- [ ] **0:20 - 0:30 | Action Generation:** Convert the winning stickies directly into Jira tracked actions (ensure assignees and sprint targets are set before ending the call).
